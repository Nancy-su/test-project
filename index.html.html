    • Muslim clothing

    Rules of hijab for men According to the Qur'an, both men and women should dress in ways that are halaal, meaning permissible, throughout their lives. According to the traditional view in Sunni Islam, men must cover from their belly buttons to their knees, though they differ on whether this includes covering the navel and knees or only what is between them. It is also mentioned in the Qur'an that it is haraam for men to wear clothing articles made of silk or animal skinned that has not been tanned. Conversely, men are permitted to wear anything made of wool, camel hair, or goat hair.     Ramadan

    During Ramadan, Muslims abstain from eating any food, drinking any liquids, smoking cigarettes, and engaging in any sexual activity, from dawn to sunset. That includes taking medication (even if you swallow a pill dry, without drinking any water)
